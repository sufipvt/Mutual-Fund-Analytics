## 1. NAV Trend Analysis

**Observation**

The daily NAV of the selected mutual fund schemes showed an overall upward trend between 2022 and 2026. Temporary declines were observed during market correction periods, but most funds recovered and continued their long-term growth.

`01_nav_analysis.ipynb`

## 2. Assets Under Management (AUM)

**Observation**

Assets Under Management increased consistently across all major fund houses from 2022 to 2025. SBI Mutual Fund maintained the highest AUM throughout the observed period, indicating its strong market presence.

**Chart**

`02_aum_analysis.ipynb`

## 3. SIP Inflow Trend

**Observation**

Monthly SIP inflows increased steadily over the four-year period and reached an all-time high of approximately ₹31,002 crore in December 2025, reflecting increasing investor participation.

# Conclusion

The exploratory analysis shows strong growth in the Indian mutual fund industry between 2022 and 2025. Rising SIP inflows, increasing AUM, expanding folio counts, and positive NAV growth indicate growing investor confidence. Demographic and geographic analyses reveal where participation is concentrated, while sector allocation and return correlation provide insights into portfolio composition and diversification.
